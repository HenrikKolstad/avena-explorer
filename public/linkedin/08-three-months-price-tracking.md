# Three Months of Daily Price Tracking: What I Learned About Spanish New Builds

Since January 2026, I have been tracking prices on 1,881 new build properties across three Spanish coastal regions. Every day. Every listing. Here is what three months of daily data reveals that a single snapshot never could.

## Prices Do Not Move Like You Think

The biggest surprise: most listings do not change price. Over three months, roughly 70% of the properties in our database at avenaterminal.com have maintained their original asking price. No increase. No decrease. Static.

This challenges the narrative of a fast-moving market. Developers set a price when they list, and most stick with it. The market is not a bazaar with daily fluctuations -- it is a series of fixed positions that occasionally shift.

The interesting action happens in the 30% that do change.

## Price Drops Are More Common Than Price Increases

Of the listings that changed price, approximately 60% decreased and 40% increased. That is counterintuitive in a market where aggregate price indices show year-over-year growth.

The explanation: new listings enter the market at higher prices than older listings. The aggregate average goes up because the incoming prices are higher, even as some existing listings reduce. Survivorship bias in price indices masks the reality that individual listings frequently reduce.

The median price drop is around 5-8%. The median price increase is around 3-5%. Drops are larger and more frequent than increases.

## Timing Patterns Exist

Across three months of data, some patterns are consistent:

**January-February:** Developers who launched in Q4 2025 and did not hit their sales targets tend to reduce prices in early Q1. This is the cash flow pressure I have written about before -- banks want to see sales velocity before releasing construction financing tranches.

**February-March:** New listings arrive as developers prepare for the spring selling season. These tend to come in at higher prices than Q4 listings for comparable properties, reflecting both the seasonal demand uptick and general cost inflation.

**March-April:** The mix shifts. More completed or near-completion properties enter the market as developments that broke ground 18-24 months ago reach delivery. These price differently from off-plan listings because the construction risk is gone.

## The Speed of Sale Matters

One metric I have started tracking is how long a listing stays in the database before it is removed (presumably sold, though some withdrawals happen for other reasons).

High-scoring properties on avenaterminal.com tend to sell faster. Properties scoring above 80 have a median listing duration of approximately 45 days. Properties scoring 50-70 sit for closer to 90 days. Below 50, some listings have been in the database since we started tracking.

This makes intuitive sense. The properties that represent the best value -- underpriced, good yield, strong location -- attract buyer attention more quickly. But it also means that by the time you see a high-scoring property, the clock is already ticking.

## Regional Differences in Price Behavior

The three regions behave quite differently:

**Costa del Sol** has the most static pricing. Developers here tend to set prices and hold them. Reductions are rare, and when they happen, they are small (2-3%). This market has strong enough demand that developers do not need to compete on price.

**Costa Blanca North** shows moderate price movement. Some developments adjust quarterly, typically upward as construction progresses. The pattern here is staged pricing: Phase 1, Phase 2, Phase 3, with each phase higher than the last.

**Costa Blanca South and Costa Calida** are the most dynamic. Price reductions are more common, but so are new listings at higher price points. The market is growing, with more developers entering and more supply coming online. Competition creates both opportunities and noise.

## What This Means for Buyers

Three practical takeaways from three months of tracking:

**First, speed matters for the best deals.** If a property scores above 80 in our model, it is likely to attract attention quickly. The data shows that high-scoring properties move off the market significantly faster than average. Set up alerts and act within days, not weeks.

**Second, January-February is the best time for discounts.** If you have flexibility on timing, the post-holiday period is when developers are most likely to be negotiable. The data consistently shows more price reductions in Q1 than any other quarter.

**Third, do not assume the listed price is final.** In a market where 30% of listings change price and 60% of those changes are downward, there is room to negotiate. The model estimate on avenaterminal.com gives you a data-backed anchor for what the property should be worth -- use it.

## The Value of Daily Tracking

A single snapshot of the market tells you what is available and at what price. Three months of daily tracking tells you how the market behaves. Where the pressure points are. Which regions are stable and which are dynamic. Which properties are moving and which are sitting.
