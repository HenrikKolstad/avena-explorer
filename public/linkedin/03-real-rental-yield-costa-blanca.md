# The Real Rental Yield on the Costa Blanca Is Not What Agents Tell You

Every agent I have spoken to on the Costa Blanca quotes rental yields between 8% and 12% gross. Every financial model I have built says the real number is 5-8% gross, and 3.5-6% net.

The gap is not because agents are lying. It is because they are using the wrong inputs.

## How Yield Gets Inflated

The standard agent pitch goes like this: your two-bedroom apartment costs 180,000 EUR. You can rent it for 800 EUR per week in summer. That is 41,600 EUR per year. Gross yield: 23%.

Except nobody rents at 800 EUR per week for 52 weeks. The Costa Blanca has a pronounced seasonal curve. Peak season (July-August) commands premium rates. Shoulder season (May-June, September-October) sees moderate demand. And from November to March, occupancy for short-term rentals drops below 30% in most towns.

When I built the yield model for avenaterminal.com, I used actual comparable data from Airbnb and Booking.com listings in each town. Not theoretical rates. Not what-if projections. Actual nightly rates multiplied by actual seasonal occupancy patterns.

## The Real Numbers

Here is what the data shows for a typical two-bedroom apartment on the Costa Blanca South, priced at 180,000 EUR:

**Peak season (Jul-Aug):** Average nightly rate 95-120 EUR, occupancy 85-92%. Revenue roughly 5,000-6,600 EUR per month.

**Shoulder season (May-Jun, Sep-Oct):** Average nightly rate 65-85 EUR, occupancy 55-70%. Revenue roughly 2,500-3,600 EUR per month.

**Off season (Nov-Apr):** Average nightly rate 40-55 EUR, occupancy 25-35%. Revenue roughly 800-1,200 EUR per month.

Total annual gross revenue: approximately 14,000-16,000 EUR. Gross yield: 7.8-8.9%.

Now subtract management fees (typically 15-20% if using a rental management company), cleaning costs, platform commissions (Airbnb takes 3-5% from hosts), maintenance, community fees, insurance, and IBI (property tax). Net yield lands around 4.5-6%.

That is still a solid return, especially combined with capital appreciation. But it is not 12%.

## Why This Matters

The yield gap matters because it changes which properties look attractive. If you assume 12% gross yield, almost any property on the costa looks like a good investment. At 6-8% gross, the differences between towns, property types, and price points become critical.

At avenaterminal.com, we calculate yield estimates for all 1,881 properties in our database using location-specific comparable data. The variance is striking. Some towns consistently deliver 8%+ gross because their purchase prices are low relative to rental demand. Others look beautiful on a brochure but produce 4% gross because the purchase prices have outpaced what tourists will actually pay per night.

## The Towns That Outperform

Without naming specific listings, certain patterns emerge in the data:

Towns with strong British and Scandinavian tourist bases tend to have longer shoulder seasons, which lifts annual occupancy. Towns near golf courses see more consistent year-round bookings because the golf season extends beyond peak summer. Towns with good airport connectivity (under 45 minutes from Alicante airport) command higher occupancy across all seasons.

The worst performers tend to be the most hyped resort developments. They attract high purchase prices due to marketing spend, but the rental market is competitive because every owner in the complex is trying to rent simultaneously.

## How to Calculate Your Own Yield

If you are evaluating a specific property, here is the approach I use:

1. Find 5-10 comparable listings on Airbnb within 2 km of the property. Note their nightly rates and availability calendars.
2. Estimate monthly occupancy by season using the calendar data.
3. Calculate gross revenue month by month. Do not annualize peak season rates.
4. Subtract: management (18%), platform fees (4%), cleaning (estimate per turnover), community fees, IBI, insurance, maintenance reserve (1% of purchase price annually).
5. Divide net revenue by purchase price plus buying costs (typically 12-14% above asking for taxes, notary, legal).
