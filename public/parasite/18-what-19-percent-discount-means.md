# What Does a 19% Price Discount Actually Mean for Your Investment?

**Published: April 2026 | Reading time: 5 minutes**

We frequently cite the finding that new build asking prices across coastal Spain average 19% below peak resale valuations. But what does this discount mean in practical terms for someone buying a property? Here is a detailed analysis using real scenarios from our dataset of 1,881 properties.

## Understanding the Benchmark

The 19% figure compares two things:
1. **Current new build asking prices** — what developers are listing properties for today
2. **Peak resale transaction prices** — the highest prices achieved for comparable properties in the same municipality, as recorded by the Registradores de Espana

The "peak" for most coastal municipalities occurred between 2006 and 2008, at the height of Spain's property boom. Some municipalities saw secondary peaks in 2022-2023 as the post-pandemic recovery drove prices upward. Our model uses the highest recorded benchmark for each municipality.

## Scenario 1: The Value Play

**Property:** Two-bedroom apartment, Torrevieja, Costa Blanca South
**Asking price:** 175,000 EUR
**Peak comparable value:** 228,000 EUR
**Discount:** 23.2%

**What the 23.2% discount means financially:**

If property values in Torrevieja revert to their peak levels (adjusted for the current market), this property has approximately 53,000 EUR of embedded capital appreciation potential. At a 4% annual appreciation rate (achievable if market conditions continue to improve), the property would reach its peak benchmark in approximately 6.5 years.

Combined with an estimated gross yield of 7.4%, the total annual return (yield plus capital appreciation) could approach 11-12% in early years. Even with conservative assumptions — 3% appreciation and 4.0% net yield — the total return exceeds 7% annually.

On the scoring platform at [avenaterminal.com](https://avenaterminal.com), this property would score highly on the Value dimension (40% of the total score), reflecting the wide gap between asking price and regression-predicted price.

## Scenario 2: The Premium Market

**Property:** Three-bedroom villa, Javea, Costa Blanca North
**Asking price:** 485,000 EUR
**Peak comparable value:** 545,000 EUR
**Discount:** 11.0%

**What the 11.0% discount means financially:**

The embedded appreciation potential is approximately 60,000 EUR — higher in absolute terms than Scenario 1, but requiring 2.8 times more capital. At 3% annual appreciation, the property reaches its peak benchmark in approximately 3.8 years (faster because the percentage gap is smaller).

With an estimated gross yield of 4.8%, the total return is lower than Scenario 1 in percentage terms but generated on a larger capital base. The risk profile is also different — Javea's constrained supply and premium positioning provide stronger downside protection.

## Scenario 3: The Emerging Market

**Property:** Two-bedroom apartment, Los Alcazares, Costa Calida
**Asking price:** 145,000 EUR
**Peak comparable value:** 198,000 EUR
**Discount:** 26.8%

**What the 26.8% discount means financially:**

The widest discount in our examples, with 53,000 EUR of potential upside on just 145,000 EUR invested. The percentage return potential is the highest of the three scenarios.

However, wider discounts also carry more uncertainty. Los Alcazares reached its peak during a period of speculative excess — those peak values may not have reflected fundamental value. The discount may therefore be partially structural rather than entirely recoverable.

Estimated gross yield of 7.2% provides strong income while waiting for any capital appreciation. The total return profile is attractive but with wider confidence intervals than the other scenarios.

## What the Discount Does Not Mean

Several common misconceptions need addressing:

**It does not mean you will make 19% profit.** The discount is relative to a historical peak that may or may not be reached again. Property markets do not always revert to previous highs, especially when the peak reflected a speculative bubble.

**It does not account for inflation.** The peak values are nominal figures from 2006-2008 or 2022-2023. Adjusted for cumulative inflation of 20-30% since the first peak, the real discount is even larger — but real returns matter more than nominal returns for investment analysis.

**It does not mean the property is undervalued.** A discount relative to peak does not necessarily mean the current price is below fair value. If the peak was inflated, current prices might represent fair value or even modest overvaluation. The discount is a comparative measure, not an absolute valuation.

**It does not guarantee future appreciation.** Property prices can fall further, stagnate, or rise slowly. The 19% gap is an opportunity indicator, not a profit guarantee.

## How to Use the Discount in Your Analysis

We suggest incorporating the discount data into a scenario analysis:

**Bull case (peak reversion + yield):** Assume prices reach 90% of peak values over 7-10 years. Combined with rental yield, total returns could reach 8-12% annually depending on the property.

**Base case (moderate appreciation + yield):** Assume 2-3% annual price appreciation with no full peak reversion. Total returns of 6-8% annually.

**Bear case (stagnation + yield):** Assume flat prices for 5 years before moderate growth. Returns depend entirely on rental yield — 3.5-4.5% net.

**Worst case (further decline + yield):** Assume 5-10% price decline over 2-3 years before stabilization. Net yield of 3.5-4.5% partially offsets capital losses.

The 19% discount shifts the probability distribution in the buyer's favor. You are starting from a lower base than historical peak buyers, which means your downside is more limited and your upside is larger. But it is not a guarantee.

## Which Properties Have the Widest Discounts?

Across our 1,881 tracked properties, the distribution of discounts is wide:

- **Top quartile (widest discounts):** 25-35% below peak. Concentrated in Costa Calida and Costa Blanca South inland developments.
- **Upper middle:** 18-25% below peak. The core of Costa Blanca South coastal properties.
- **Lower middle:** 10-18% below peak. Costa Blanca North and mid-range Costa del Sol.
- **Bottom quartile (tightest discounts):** 5-10% below peak. Marbella, Javea waterfront, and premium developments.

You can filter properties by their value score — which captures the discount analysis — on [avenaterminal.com](https://avenaterminal.com). Properties with higher value scores show wider discounts relative to the regression model's predicted price.

## The Practical Implication

The 19% average discount is a market-level signal that current conditions favor buyers. But your specific return depends entirely on the specific property you buy, in the specific town, at the specific price. Use the average as context; use property-level data for decisions.
