# Spain Property Tax for Foreigners in 2026: The Complete Breakdown

**Published: April 2026 | Reading time: 6 minutes**

Buying property in Spain as a foreigner involves taxes and fees that can add 10-15% to your purchase price. Most guides give you vague ranges. Using data from 1,881 new build transactions we track, here are the actual numbers for 2026.

## Purchase Taxes: New Build vs Resale

This is the most important distinction. New builds and resale properties are taxed entirely differently in Spain.

**New builds (what we track):**
- **IVA (VAT):** 10% of the purchase price. This is a flat national rate with no regional variation. On a 250,000 EUR property, that is 25,000 EUR.
- **AJD (Stamp Duty):** Varies by autonomous community. In Valencia (covering Costa Blanca): 1.5%. In Murcia (Costa Calida): 1.5%. In Andalucia (Costa del Sol): 1.2%. On a 250,000 EUR property in Valencia, that is 3,750 EUR.

**Resale properties:**
- **ITP (Transfer Tax):** Replaces IVA and AJD. Rate varies by region: Valencia 10%, Murcia 8%, Andalucia 7%. On a 250,000 EUR resale property in Valencia, that is 25,000 EUR.

The net effect: new builds in Valencia cost approximately 11.5% in purchase tax versus 10% for resale. In Andalucia, new builds cost 11.2% versus 7% for resale. This means new builds carry a higher tax burden in Andalucia, which partially offsets the 19% average price discount we identify in our data on [avenaterminal.com](https://avenaterminal.com).

## Annual Property Taxes

Once you own the property, several recurring taxes apply:

**IBI (Impuesto sobre Bienes Inmuebles):** Spain's equivalent of council tax or property tax. Calculated on the cadastral value (valor catastral), which is typically 30-50% of the market value. Rates vary by municipality from 0.4% to 1.1% of cadastral value. For a new build with a market value of 250,000 EUR and a cadastral value of 100,000 EUR, expect to pay 400-1,100 EUR per year.

**Basura (Rubbish collection):** A separate annual charge ranging from 50 to 300 EUR depending on the municipality. Not technically a tax, but unavoidable.

**IRNR (Non-Resident Income Tax):** Even if you do not rent the property out, Spanish tax law imputes rental income to non-resident owners. The imputed income is calculated as 1.1% of the cadastral value (or 2% if the cadastral value has not been revised since 1994). Tax rate for EU/EEA residents: 19%. Tax rate for non-EU residents: 24%.

For a property with a cadastral value of 100,000 EUR: imputed income = 1,100 EUR, tax for EU residents = 209 EUR per year. This is the minimum you pay even if the property sits empty.

## Rental Income Tax for Non-Residents

If you do rent the property out, the tax treatment depends on your residency status:

**EU/EEA residents:** Taxed at 19% on net rental income (after deductible expenses including mortgage interest, repairs, insurance, community fees, management fees, depreciation, and IBI). This is a favourable regime that allows you to offset genuine expenses.

**Non-EU residents (including post-Brexit British citizens):** Taxed at 24% on gross rental income with no expense deductions. This is a significantly worse deal. On rental income of 15,000 EUR per year, a UK resident pays 3,600 EUR in tax while a Norwegian resident pays approximately 1,710 EUR (19% on net income after deductions).

This tax difference is significant enough to affect investment returns. Our rental yield estimates on [avenaterminal.com](https://avenaterminal.com) show gross figures — your net yield after tax will vary based on your residency status and applicable tax treaty.

## Capital Gains Tax

When you sell, capital gains tax applies:

**Rate:** 19% on gains up to 6,000 EUR, 21% on gains from 6,001 to 50,000 EUR, 23% on gains from 50,001 to 200,000 EUR, and 27% on gains above 200,000 EUR.

**Retention:** The buyer is required to withhold 3% of the sale price and pay it directly to the tax authorities as an advance payment on the seller's capital gains liability. This means you receive 97% of the sale price at completion and must file a tax return to either pay additional tax or reclaim the excess.

**Inflation adjustment:** Spain does not index capital gains for inflation, meaning you pay tax on nominal gains including the inflation component. On a property held for 10 years with 25% cumulative inflation, a significant portion of your "gain" is simply inflation, yet it is fully taxable.

## Hidden Costs Most Guides Ignore

**Notary fees:** 600-1,200 EUR depending on the property price and complexity.

**Land registry fees:** 400-700 EUR.

**Legal fees:** Budget 1-1.5% of purchase price for an independent lawyer. Never use the developer's lawyer.

**Bank charges:** If you open a Spanish bank account (recommended), expect setup fees of 0-200 EUR and annual maintenance of 50-200 EUR.

**NIE application:** The Numero de Identificacion de Extranjero is mandatory for any property transaction. Application is free but processing through a gestor costs 100-250 EUR.

**Mortgage costs:** If financing, add valuation fees (300-600 EUR), mortgage arrangement fees (0.5-1.5% of the loan), and mortgage tax (already included in the AJD rate for new builds).

## What Changed in 2026

The main changes affecting foreign buyers in 2026:

**Digital NIE processing** is now available in some consulates, reducing wait times from weeks to days.

**Anti-fraud measures** require more documentation on the source of funds, particularly for cash purchases above 100,000 EUR. Non-EU buyers should prepare bank statements and proof of funds well in advance.

**Municipal IBI rates** have increased in several popular expat towns, reflecting higher cadastral values from recent revaluations.
