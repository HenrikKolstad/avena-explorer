# Why Costa del Sol New Builds Command a Premium (And Whether It's Worth Paying)

**Published: April 2026 | Reading time: 5 minutes**

Costa del Sol new builds cost 40-60% more per square metre than equivalent properties on Costa Blanca or Costa Calida. Our data across 1,881 tracked properties confirms this premium is real and consistent. The question is whether it is justified.

## The Premium in Numbers

From our dataset on [avenaterminal.com](https://avenaterminal.com):

- **Costa del Sol average:** 3,100 EUR/m2
- **Costa Blanca North average:** 2,650 EUR/m2
- **Costa Blanca South average:** 1,850 EUR/m2
- **Costa Calida average:** 1,450 EUR/m2

On a standard 90 m2 two-bedroom apartment, the Costa del Sol premium translates to:
- vs Costa Blanca South: 112,500 EUR more
- vs Costa Calida: 148,500 EUR more

These are significant sums that need to justify themselves through either superior rental income, capital appreciation, or quality of life.

## What You Get for the Premium

### Brand Value
Costa del Sol is the most recognized Spanish coastal brand internationally. "Marbella" is a global luxury signifier in a way that "Torrevieja" is not. This brand recognition translates to three tangible benefits:

**Wider buyer pool on resale.** When you sell, your potential buyer pool includes wealthy individuals from the Middle East, North America, and Asia who may never consider Costa Blanca. This liquidity premium supports prices.

**Higher rental rates from premium tourists.** Costa del Sol attracts higher-spending tourists who book through premium channels. A well-positioned two-bedroom apartment in Marbella can command 1,200-1,800 EUR per week in peak season versus 650-900 EUR in Costa Blanca South.

**Year-round demand.** The Costa del Sol brand extends beyond summer. Golf tourism, business conferences, and lifestyle tourism maintain demand through winter months, extending the rentable season.

### Infrastructure
Malaga airport handled 22.5 million passengers in 2025 with connections to over 130 destinations — significantly more than Alicante's 16.8 million. The AVE high-speed train connects Malaga to Madrid in 2.5 hours. The motorway network is generally superior.

Healthcare, international schools, high-end retail, and dining options are more abundant on the Costa del Sol than any other Spanish coastal region.

### Climate
The Costa del Sol genuinely earns its name. Average annual sunshine hours are approximately 320 days, marginally better than Costa Blanca (305 days). More significantly, winter temperatures average 2-3 degrees warmer, extending the outdoor living season.

### Developer Quality
Major international developers are more active on the Costa del Sol. Taylor Wimpey, Karl Lagerfeld's branded residences, and luxury boutique developers all operate here. The average specification of new builds is higher, with more premium fixtures, smart home technology, and architectural design.

## What You Lose

### Yield
The premium purchase price crushes rental yields. Average estimated gross yield for Costa del Sol new builds: 5.1%. Compare that to Costa Blanca South at 6.8% or Costa Calida at 7.0%.

On a 400,000 EUR Costa del Sol apartment yielding 5.1% gross, your annual rental income is approximately 20,400 EUR. On a 220,000 EUR Costa Blanca South apartment yielding 6.8% gross, your annual rental income is approximately 14,960 EUR. You invest 180,000 EUR more capital for only 5,440 EUR more annual income. The marginal return on the additional capital is just 3.0%.

### Discount Opportunity
The 19% average discount below peak prices that characterizes our full dataset narrows to 10-15% on the Costa del Sol. If part of your investment thesis relies on buying below fair value and benefiting from price recovery, there is less room for this on the Sol.

### Price Risk
Higher absolute prices mean greater nominal exposure. A 10% price decline on a 400,000 EUR property costs you 40,000 EUR versus 22,000 EUR on a 220,000 EUR property.

## Who Should Pay the Premium?

The data from our analysis of 1,881 properties suggests the Costa del Sol premium is justified for:

**Lifestyle-first buyers** who will use the property extensively and value the infrastructure, dining, and cultural offerings that the premium towns provide.

**Ultra-high-budget buyers** (above 800,000 EUR) where the Costa del Sol offers unique products — beachfront penthouses, golf-front villas, branded residences — that simply do not exist elsewhere on the Spanish coast.

**Resale-focused investors** with a 5-10 year horizon who believe the brand premium will appreciate as global demand for Mediterranean luxury property grows.

The premium is harder to justify for:

**Yield-focused investors** who will rarely visit the property and whose primary metric is annual return on capital.

**Budget-constrained buyers** for whom the premium consumes capital that could be deployed more productively in a higher-yielding market.

**First-time buyers** who would benefit from a lower-risk entry point to learn the Spanish property market.

## Town-Level Analysis

Not all Costa del Sol towns carry the same premium:

**Marbella:** Maximum premium. 4,500-6,000 EUR/m2 for new builds. Justified only for lifestyle or luxury positioning. Yields: 3.8-4.5% gross.

**Estepona:** Growing premium. 2,800-3,500 EUR/m2. Better value than Marbella with improving infrastructure. Yields: 4.8-5.5% gross. Currently the strongest value proposition on the Costa del Sol.

**Fuengirola:** Moderate premium. 2,600-3,200 EUR/m2. Strong rental market with year-round demand. Yields: 5.2-5.8% gross.

**Benalmadena:** Entry point to Costa del Sol. 2,400-3,000 EUR/m2. Good yields at 5.5-6.0% gross but less prestigious branding.

**Manilva/Casares:** Budget Costa del Sol. 2,100-2,600 EUR/m2. Prices approaching Costa Blanca levels but with less established rental demand.
