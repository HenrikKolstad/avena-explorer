# Best Towns for Rental Yield Under 300K in Coastal Spain

**Published: April 2026 | Reading time: 5 minutes**

If your budget caps at 300,000 EUR and your goal is rental yield, the town you choose matters more than the property itself. Using our dataset of 1,881 new build properties across 100 towns in coastal Spain, we ranked municipalities by estimated gross rental yield for properties priced under 300,000 EUR. Here are the results.

## The Top 10 by Gross Yield

Based on data from [avenaterminal.com](https://avenaterminal.com), these towns offer the strongest estimated gross yields for new build properties under 300,000 EUR:

### 1. Torrevieja — 7.4% Average Gross Yield
Average purchase price: 175,000 EUR. Torrevieja dominates the yield rankings because of its unique combination: very affordable purchase prices, established tourist infrastructure, and a large expatriate community that creates year-round rental demand. The salt lakes, beaches, and commercial facilities attract both summer tourists and long-stay winter visitors. Properties available from 95,000 EUR.

### 2. Los Alcazares — 7.2% Average Gross Yield
Average purchase price: 165,000 EUR. Sitting on the Mar Menor lagoon, Los Alcazares benefits from the warmest water on the Spanish coast, making it popular for families and water sports enthusiasts. The town has invested heavily in its promenade and beach facilities. Smaller than Torrevieja but with proportionally strong rental demand.

### 3. San Pedro del Pinatar — 7.0% Average Gross Yield
Average purchase price: 155,000 EUR. Adjacent to Los Alcazares on the Mar Menor, San Pedro offers similarly affordable pricing with a more authentic Spanish atmosphere. The town's mud baths and salt flats attract health tourists, adding a niche rental market beyond standard holiday lets. Lo Pagan, its coastal district, is the primary rental area.

### 4. Pilar de la Horadada — 6.8% Average Gross Yield
Average purchase price: 195,000 EUR. The southernmost point of the Alicante province, just before crossing into Murcia. Pilar benefits from newer infrastructure and less density than Torrevieja. Torre de la Horadada on the coast has excellent beaches and is increasingly popular with Scandinavian renters.

### 5. Orihuela Costa — 6.4% Average Gross Yield
Average purchase price: 235,000 EUR. Higher entry price than the top four but compensated by premium rental rates. La Zenia, Cabo Roig, and Playa Flamenca are well-established rental destinations. The proximity to La Zenia Boulevard commercial centre adds year-round appeal.

### 6. Guardamar del Segura — 6.3% Average Gross Yield
Average purchase price: 210,000 EUR. Beautiful pine-forest dunes, a long sandy beach, and a charming town centre make Guardamar increasingly popular. New build activity has increased, bringing modern stock to a market previously dominated by older resale apartments.

### 7. Santa Pola — 6.1% Average Gross Yield
Average purchase price: 225,000 EUR. A working fishing town with authentic character and excellent seafood restaurants. Santa Pola draws Spanish domestic tourists as well as international visitors, providing a broader demand base than purely expat-oriented towns.

### 8. Gran Alacant — 6.0% Average Gross Yield
Average purchase price: 200,000 EUR. A residential area between Santa Pola and Alicante city, Gran Alacant offers the closest new build options to Alicante airport (15 minutes). The Carabasi beach (cliffs and dunes) provides a distinctive setting. Proximity to Alicante city adds an urban rental market beyond tourism.

### 9. Benidorm — 5.9% Average Gross Yield
Average purchase price: 195,000 EUR. Benidorm's high-rise model means apartments are compact and relatively affordable. The town's entertainment infrastructure, long beaches, and massive tourist volumes drive consistent rental demand year-round. The British and Spanish domestic markets dominate bookings.

### 10. Ciudad Quesada — 5.8% Average Gross Yield
Average purchase price: 215,000 EUR. An inland urbanization between Torrevieja and Orihuela Costa, Ciudad Quesada offers larger properties (villas and townhouses) at lower prices than coastal equivalents. The trade-off is distance from the beach (10-15 minutes by car), which limits peak-season rental rates but appeals to long-stay tenants who prioritize space and amenities.

## What Drives Yield Variation

Across these 10 towns, several patterns emerge from the data:

**Purchase price is the dominant factor.** Lower purchase prices drive higher yields more consistently than higher rental rates. A 150,000 EUR apartment renting for 9,000 EUR annually (6.0% gross) outperforms a 300,000 EUR apartment renting for 16,500 EUR annually (5.5% gross), even though the absolute rental income is higher in the second case.

**Rental market depth matters.** Towns with 200+ active short-term rental listings on major platforms show more consistent yields than towns with fewer than 50 listings. Deeper markets mean more reviews, better search visibility, and more reliable occupancy rates.

**Airport proximity helps.** Every one of the top 10 towns is within 60 minutes of either Alicante or Murcia airport. Ease of access translates directly to booking frequency.

**Seasonality varies significantly.** Torrevieja and Benidorm show 180-200 bookable nights per year. Smaller towns like San Pedro del Pinatar may achieve 130-160 nights. The longer season in larger towns partly compensates for their slightly higher purchase prices.

## Budget Allocation Strategy

With a 300,000 EUR total budget (including acquisition costs of approximately 13%), your net purchase budget is approximately 265,000 EUR. Here is how we would allocate across different strategies:

**Maximum yield:** Buy two apartments in Torrevieja or Los Alcazares at 130,000-135,000 EUR each. Combined gross yield potential: 7.0-7.5%. Diversification across two units reduces vacancy risk.

**Balanced yield and appreciation:** Buy one two-bedroom apartment in Orihuela Costa at 230,000-260,000 EUR. Lower yield (6.2-6.5% gross) but stronger capital appreciation potential and a property that appeals to a higher-spending tenant demographic.

**Lifestyle plus yield:** Buy one property in Guardamar or Santa Pola at 200,000-250,000 EUR. These towns offer genuine Spanish character that you will enjoy during personal visits, while still delivering solid rental returns.

## Warning Signs

Not every cheap property generates good yields. Watch for:

**Towns with tourist license restrictions.** Some municipalities are limiting new short-term rental licenses. Check before buying.

**Properties far from amenities.** A cheap apartment 3 kilometres from the beach and shops will struggle to attract bookings regardless of the headline yield calculation.

**Oversupplied developments.** If a development has 200 identical apartments, you are competing with your own neighbours for the same tenant pool. Look for smaller developments with differentiation.
