# New Build vs Resale Property in Spain: What the Data Shows

**Published: April 2026 | Reading time: 5 minutes**

The question comes up in every property forum: should you buy new or resale in Spain? Both sides have vocal advocates. We decided to examine the question through data rather than opinion, drawing on our dataset of 1,881 new build properties and publicly available resale market data.

## The Price Gap

New builds in coastal Spain cost more per square metre than resale properties — but the gap is narrower than many assume. Based on our tracked data and Registradores benchmarks:

- **New build average:** approximately 2,180 EUR/m2
- **Resale average (coastal equivalent):** approximately 1,750 EUR/m2
- **New build premium:** approximately 24.5%

This premium reflects several tangible benefits: modern construction standards, energy efficiency, developer warranty (10-year structural guarantee), new installations, and current building regulations compliance. Whether the premium is worth paying depends on your priorities and holding period.

## The Tax Difference

This is often overlooked but significantly affects total cost:

**New build taxes:** 10% IVA + 1.2-1.5% AJD = 11.2-11.5% total
**Resale taxes:** 7-10% ITP (varies by region)

In Andalucia, the difference is stark: new builds cost 11.2% in tax while resale costs 7%, a 4.2 percentage point difference. On a 300,000 EUR property, that is 12,600 EUR more in taxes for the new build.

In Valencia, the gap is narrower: 11.5% for new builds versus 10% for resale. In Murcia: 11.5% versus 8%.

When comparing prices, always factor in the total acquisition cost including taxes. A new build listed at 300,000 EUR costs approximately 333,600 EUR all-in (Valencia), while a resale at 300,000 EUR costs approximately 330,000 EUR all-in. The effective premium shrinks.

## Energy Efficiency

New builds in Spain must comply with current CTE (Codigo Tecnico de la Edificacion) standards, which require significantly better insulation, window performance, and energy systems than older construction. Our data from [avenaterminal.com](https://avenaterminal.com) shows:

- **New builds with A or B rating:** 62% of our dataset
- **Resale properties with A or B rating:** estimated 8-12% of coastal resale stock

The practical impact: a new build with an A or B energy rating will cost approximately 40-60% less to heat and cool than a typical resale property built before 2006. For a property used year-round, this translates to 800-1,500 EUR per year in energy savings. Over a 10-year holding period, that is 8,000-15,000 EUR — a meaningful offset against the purchase price premium.

## Rental Performance

For rental investors, new builds offer measurable advantages:

**Higher nightly rates.** New builds command a 15-20% premium in rental rates over comparable resale properties in the same location. Modern kitchens, air conditioning, and communal pools are expected by short-term renters, and new builds deliver these as standard.

**Lower maintenance costs.** For the first 5-7 years, a new build requires minimal maintenance expenditure. Resale properties often need 5,000-15,000 EUR in updates (kitchen, bathrooms, air conditioning) to compete effectively in the rental market.

**Better guest reviews.** Rental platforms prioritize properties with high review scores. New builds consistently receive better ratings for cleanliness, amenities, and overall condition, which drives higher visibility and booking rates.

Our yield estimates across 1,881 new build properties show an average gross yield of 6.2%. Comparable resale properties in the same towns typically yield 5.5-6.0% gross, but after accounting for the renovation costs needed to compete, the effective yield advantage of new builds is significant.

## Warranty Protection

Spanish law provides substantial warranty protection for new builds:

- **1 year:** All defects (finishing, installations, fixtures)
- **3 years:** Habitability defects (plumbing, electrical, insulation)
- **10 years:** Structural defects (foundation, load-bearing elements)

These warranties are backed by mandatory insurance (seguro decenal) that the developer must purchase before construction begins. If the developer goes bankrupt, the insurance still covers structural claims for the full 10-year period.

Resale properties come with no warranty unless the seller provides one voluntarily (rare) or the property is still within its original warranty period.

## The Customization Factor

Buying off-plan or in early construction phases allows you to customize finishes, layouts, and sometimes floor plans. This is not possible with resale. For buyers with specific requirements — accessibility modifications, home office spaces, particular kitchen layouts — new builds offer flexibility that resale cannot match.

## Where Resale Wins

It would be dishonest not to acknowledge where resale properties hold advantages:

**Location.** The best locations in established towns are already built up. New developments are often on the periphery or in less established areas. If you want a specific street or a front-line beach position, resale may be your only option.

**Immediate availability.** New builds, especially off-plan, may not be ready for 12-24 months. Resale properties are available immediately.

**Negotiation room.** Resale sellers are typically more flexible on price than developers. A 10-15% discount on asking price is common in negotiations, while developers may offer 3-5% or throw in extras (furniture packages, appliance upgrades) rather than reducing the headline price.

**Established communities.** Resale properties are in completed communities where you can see exactly who your neighbours are, how well the community is maintained, and what the atmosphere is like. New developments are a gamble on future community dynamics.

## The Data-Driven Answer

Our analysis suggests new builds offer better risk-adjusted returns for investment buyers, primarily due to lower maintenance costs, higher rental rates, energy efficiency, and warranty protection. The purchase price premium is real but is partially offset by these operational advantages.

For lifestyle buyers who plan to live in the property, the answer is more nuanced. If location trumps all other factors, resale may be necessary. If modern amenities, energy efficiency, and minimal maintenance matter more, new builds are the stronger choice.
