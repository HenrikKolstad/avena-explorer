# Getting a Spanish Mortgage as a Non-Resident in 2026

**Published: April 2026 | Reading time: 5 minutes**

Financing a Spanish property as a non-resident is possible but different from getting a mortgage in your home country. After tracking 1,881 new build properties and the financing options available to international buyers, here is what you need to know about the current lending landscape.

## What Non-Residents Can Borrow

Spanish banks will lend non-residents up to **60-70% of the purchase price** (or valuation, whichever is lower). This is significantly less than the 80-95% LTV ratios available to Spanish residents. Some banks stretch to 75% for EU/EEA citizens with strong income profiles, but 60-65% is the standard offer.

For a 250,000 EUR property, expect to need:
- **Deposit:** 75,000-100,000 EUR (30-40%)
- **Acquisition costs:** approximately 33,000 EUR (taxes, fees, legal)
- **Total cash required:** 108,000-133,000 EUR

This means you need roughly 43-53% of the purchase price in cash. For many Northern European buyers accustomed to higher leverage, this is a significant capital requirement.

## Current Interest Rates (April 2026)

**Variable rate mortgages:** Euribor + 1.5% to 2.5%. With 12-month Euribor currently at approximately 2.8%, effective rates are 4.3-5.3%. Variable rates reset annually based on the Euribor index.

**Fixed rate mortgages:** 3.8-4.8% for 15-20 year terms. Fixed rates have come down from their 2024 peaks and offer certainty against future rate movements.

**Mixed mortgages:** Fixed for the first 2-5 years, then switching to variable. Initial fixed rates of 3.2-3.8%, reverting to Euribor + 1.8-2.2%.

For non-residents, expect rates at the higher end of these ranges. Banks price non-resident risk at approximately 0.3-0.5% above equivalent resident rates.

## Which Banks Lend to Non-Residents?

Not all Spanish banks are equally welcoming to non-resident borrowers. Based on market experience:

**Most active for non-residents:** CaixaBank, Sabadell, Unicaja, Bankinter. These banks have dedicated non-resident mortgage departments with English-speaking staff and established processes for international income verification.

**Selective:** BBVA, Santander. These major banks will lend to non-residents but tend to be slower, more bureaucratic, and less flexible on terms.

**Specialist brokers:** Several mortgage brokers specialize in non-resident lending and can access multiple banks on your behalf. Their fee (typically 0.5-1% of the loan) can be worthwhile for the time saved and the competitive rate shopping they provide.

## Documentation Required

Spanish banks require extensive documentation from non-resident borrowers:

**Identity:** Passport, NIE number
**Income proof:** Last 2-3 years of tax returns (translated and apostilled), last 3-6 months of payslips, employment contract or self-employment proof
**Assets:** Bank statements (last 6-12 months), proof of deposit funds, details of any other property owned
**Debt:** Details of any existing mortgages, loans, or credit commitments
**Property:** Developer sales contract, property specification, location details

All documents must be translated into Spanish by a sworn translator (traductor jurado). Budget 300-800 EUR for translations depending on volume.

## The Valuation Process

Spanish banks commission their own property valuation (tasacion) through an approved valuation company. The valuation fee runs 300-600 EUR and is paid by the borrower.

**Critical point:** The bank will lend based on the lower of the purchase price and the valuation. For new builds, valuations occasionally come in below the purchase price, especially for off-plan properties where the valuer is estimating future value. If the valuation is lower, you need more cash to bridge the gap.

This correlation is not guaranteed but provides a useful signal.

## Mortgage Costs

Beyond the interest rate, several costs attach to a Spanish mortgage:

**Arrangement fee:** 0-1.5% of the loan amount. Some banks waive this for larger loans or as a promotional offer.

**Valuation fee:** 300-600 EUR.

**Notary fees for the mortgage deed:** 400-800 EUR (separate from the purchase deed notary fee).

**Land registry fee for the mortgage:** 300-500 EUR.

**Mortgage tax (AJD):** Since 2019, this is paid by the bank, not the borrower.

**Life insurance:** Some banks require life insurance (seguro de vida) as a condition of the mortgage. Annual premiums run 200-600 EUR depending on age and loan amount. You can source this independently rather than accepting the bank's product.

**Home insurance:** Mandatory for mortgaged properties. 200-400 EUR annually.

**Total mortgage setup costs:** approximately 1,500-3,500 EUR on top of the deposit and acquisition costs.

## Should You Finance or Pay Cash?

This question depends on your personal financial situation, but the data informs the analysis:

**Case for financing:** If Spanish mortgage rates (4-5%) are lower than the return you can earn on your capital elsewhere, leveraging makes mathematical sense. Additionally, holding cash in your home currency while borrowing in EUR provides natural diversification. If the EUR weakens against your home currency, your debt becomes cheaper to service.

**Case for cash:** Financing adds 1,500-3,500 EUR in setup costs, creates an ongoing obligation, and reduces your net rental yield. On a 250,000 EUR property with a 60% LTV mortgage at 4.5%, annual interest payments are approximately 6,750 EUR. If gross rental income is 15,500 EUR (6.2% yield), interest alone consumes 43% of your income.

**Hybrid approach:** Finance 40-50% rather than the maximum 60-70%. This reduces interest costs while preserving capital for other investments or contingencies.

## Timeline

From application to completion, expect the mortgage process to take 6-10 weeks for non-residents. This is longer than domestic mortgages due to international document verification.

**Practical tip:** Begin the mortgage application process before you find a property. Getting a mortgage pre-approval (known as a compromiso de prestamo) gives you clarity on your budget and speeds up the purchase process when you find the right property.
