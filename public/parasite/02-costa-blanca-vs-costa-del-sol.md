# Costa Blanca vs Costa del Sol: A Data-Driven Comparison for Property Buyers

**Published: April 2026 | Reading time: 5 minutes**

The two most popular coastal regions for international property buyers in Spain are Costa Blanca and Costa del Sol. Agents in each region will tell you theirs is better. We decided to let the data settle the argument. Using 1,881 new build listings tracked across both regions, here is what the numbers actually show.

## Price Comparison

The headline difference is price. Costa del Sol new builds average approximately 385,000 EUR, while Costa Blanca averages roughly 245,000 EUR. That is a 57% premium for the Sol. On a per-square-metre basis, the gap narrows but remains significant: Costa del Sol averages around 3,100 EUR/m2 versus Costa Blanca at approximately 2,050 EUR/m2.

However, these averages mask enormous variation within each costa. In Costa Blanca North, towns like Javea and Moraira approach Costa del Sol pricing at 2,800-3,100 EUR/m2. Meanwhile, Costa del Sol has affordable pockets — Manilva and Casares offer new builds at 2,100-2,400 EUR/m2, comparable to mid-range Costa Blanca South.

The takeaway: the costa you choose matters less than the specific town.

## Rental Yield Analysis

This is where Costa Blanca pulls ahead. The average estimated gross rental yield for Costa Blanca new builds is 6.8%, compared to 5.1% for Costa del Sol. The reason is straightforward: Costa Blanca purchase prices are lower, but rental rates do not drop proportionally. A two-bedroom apartment in Torrevieja renting for 650-750 EUR per week in peak season costs 40% less to buy than a comparable unit in Fuengirola achieving similar rental rates.

Costa del Sol properties compensate with longer rental seasons. The Malaga airport serves more year-round routes, and the Costa del Sol brand attracts visitors outside the June-September peak. Properties in Marbella and Estepona report 180-200 bookable nights per year versus 140-160 for typical Costa Blanca South locations.

For pure yield-focused investors, the data from [avenaterminal.com](https://avenaterminal.com) consistently shows Costa Blanca South as the strongest region.

## The 19% Discount — Where Does It Apply?

Across our full dataset of 1,881 properties, new build asking prices average 19% below peak resale valuations. But this discount is not evenly distributed. Costa Blanca South shows the widest gap at 22-28% below peak. Costa del Sol is tighter at 10-15%, reflecting stronger demand and less developer oversupply.

This matters for capital appreciation potential. If you believe prices will revert toward historical peaks, Costa Blanca South offers more upside. If you believe Costa del Sol already prices in its premium brand value, the tighter discount may be justified by lower risk.

## Infrastructure and Accessibility

Costa del Sol benefits from Malaga airport, which handled 22.5 million passengers in 2025 and connects to over 130 destinations. Alicante airport, serving Costa Blanca, handled 16.8 million passengers with fewer direct routes from Northern European regional airports.

For buyers from the UK and Ireland, both regions are well-served. For Scandinavian buyers, Costa Blanca has stronger direct connections from Oslo, Stockholm, and Copenhagen. German and Dutch buyers are split, with excellent access to both.

Road infrastructure along Costa del Sol is generally superior, with the AP-7 motorway running the full length. Costa Blanca South has good motorway access but Costa Blanca North relies more on the N-332 coastal road, which can be congested in summer.

## Healthcare and Services

Both regions have well-established private healthcare options catering to international residents. Costa del Sol has a slight edge in English-speaking services due to the larger established expat community. Costa Blanca compensates with a well-developed Scandinavian services infrastructure, including Norwegian and Swedish medical practices in the Torrevieja-Orihuela Costa corridor.

## Property Quality Comparison

Based on our analysis of developer track records and property specifications, there is no significant quality difference between the regions at equivalent price points. Both regions feature major Spanish developers (Taylor Wimpey Espana, Grupo ASG, TM Grupo) alongside smaller local builders.

The key quality indicator in our scoring model is the energy rating. Costa del Sol new builds show a marginally higher proportion of A-rated properties (34% versus 28% in Costa Blanca), likely reflecting the higher price points enabling more premium specifications.

## Which Should You Choose?

The data suggests a clear framework:

**Choose Costa Blanca if:** your budget is under 300,000 EUR, you prioritize rental yield over capital appreciation, you prefer Scandinavian expat communities, or you want the widest selection of new build options in the most competitive pricing environment.

**Choose Costa del Sol if:** your budget exceeds 400,000 EUR, you value brand recognition and resale liquidity, you plan to use the property year-round, or you prioritize airport connectivity and infrastructure.

**The overlap zone** (300,000-400,000 EUR) is where the decision gets genuinely difficult. At this price point, Costa Blanca North and Costa del Sol's eastern end offer comparable value. Run the numbers for your specific target towns on [avenaterminal.com](https://avenaterminal.com), where you can compare town-level statistics side by side.

## The Bottom Line

Neither costa is objectively better. They serve different buyer profiles at different price points. What the data from our 1,881 tracked properties shows clearly is that within-region variation exceeds between-region variation. A well-chosen property in a strong Costa Blanca town will outperform a poorly chosen property in a prestigious Costa del Sol postcode. Focus on the town, not the costa.
