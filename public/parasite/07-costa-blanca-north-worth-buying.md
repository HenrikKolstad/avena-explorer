# Is Costa Blanca North Worth Buying in 2026? A Data Analysis

**Published: April 2026 | Reading time: 5 minutes**

Costa Blanca North — the stretch from Denia to Benidorm — is often described as the premium end of the Costa Blanca market. Higher prices, better landscapes, and more discerning buyers. But does the data support paying the premium? We analyzed the Costa Blanca North properties in our dataset of 1,881 new build listings to find out.

## The Price Premium Is Real

Costa Blanca North new builds average approximately 2,650 EUR/m2, compared to 1,850 EUR/m2 for Costa Blanca South. That is a 43% premium. In specific towns, the gap is even wider: Javea commands 2,900 EUR/m2, Moraira reaches 3,100 EUR/m2, while Torrevieja in the south sits at 1,650 EUR/m2.

On an average two-bedroom apartment of 80 m2, the difference is roughly 64,000 EUR. That is a significant sum that needs to be justified by either superior capital appreciation, higher rental income, or quality-of-life factors that have personal value to the buyer.

## The Yield Trade-Off

Here is where Costa Blanca North struggles in purely financial terms. The average estimated gross rental yield for new builds in Costa Blanca North is 5.1%, compared to 6.8% in Costa Blanca South. Higher purchase prices are not offset by proportionally higher rental rates.

A two-bedroom apartment in Javea might rent for 800-950 EUR per week in peak season, while a comparable unit in Torrevieja achieves 650-750 EUR per week. The rental rate difference is 15-25%, but the purchase price difference is 40-50%. The arithmetic does not favor the northern costa for yield-focused investors.

However, Costa Blanca North offers something the yield numbers do not capture: a longer shoulder season. The northern costa attracts more upmarket visitors who travel outside peak months, extending the bookable season by 3-5 weeks. This partially closes the yield gap but does not eliminate it.

## The Discount Is Narrower

Our data shows Costa Blanca North new builds averaging 12-18% below peak resale valuations, compared to 22-28% in Costa Blanca South. The tighter gap reflects stronger underlying demand and less developer oversupply.

The interpretation depends on your perspective. If you view the discount as an opportunity to buy below fair value, Costa Blanca South offers more upside. If you view a tighter gap as a sign of market health and lower risk, Costa Blanca North is more reassuring.

## What Makes Costa Blanca North Different

Several structural factors distinguish the northern costa from the south:

**Geography constrains supply.** The mountainous terrain between Denia and Benidorm limits development land. Unlike Costa Blanca South, where flat terrain allows large-scale developments, the northern costa is physically constrained. Limited supply supports prices long-term.

**Buyer demographics skew premium.** Costa Blanca North attracts a higher proportion of Scandinavian and Northern European buyers with larger budgets. The average purchase price in Javea is more than double the average in Torrevieja. This buyer profile creates a self-reinforcing premium.

**Municipal planning is more restrictive.** Towns like Javea, Moraira, and Altea have implemented stricter building height limits and density restrictions compared to southern towns. This preserves the aesthetic character but limits new supply, supporting prices.

**Infrastructure quality varies.** Medical facilities, road networks, and commercial amenities are generally comparable to the south, though international schools are concentrated in certain towns. The AP-7 motorway provides good north-south connectivity, but the N-332 coastal road through the northern costa is narrower and more congested than southern stretches.

## Town-by-Town Breakdown

Using data from [avenaterminal.com](https://avenaterminal.com), here is how the main Costa Blanca North towns compare:

**Javea:** The most established international market. Average new build price around 345,000 EUR. Strong resale liquidity. Limited new supply. Yield: 4.8-5.2% gross. Best for lifestyle buyers who plan to use the property themselves.

**Moraira:** The premium end. Average new build price around 420,000 EUR. Very limited new build stock. Yield: 4.2-4.8% gross. Appeals to buyers seeking exclusivity and privacy.

**Denia:** More affordable entry point. Average new build price around 265,000 EUR. Better yield potential at 5.5-6.0% gross. Strong year-round population provides a base rental market beyond tourism.

**Altea:** Artistic, upmarket village feel. Average new build price around 310,000 EUR. Limited stock. Yield: 4.5-5.0% gross. Strong appeal to culturally-oriented buyers.

**Calpe:** A more mixed market with both premium and affordable options. Average new build price around 240,000 EUR. Better yields at 5.8-6.3% gross. The Penon de Ifach gives Calpe a distinctive identity.

**Benidorm:** The outlier. Despite being in Costa Blanca North geographically, Benidorm's market dynamics resemble the south — high volumes, competitive pricing, and strong rental yields at 6.5-7.2% gross. Average new build price around 195,000 EUR.

## Who Should Buy Costa Blanca North?

The data points to clear buyer profiles:

**Buy in Costa Blanca North if:** You plan to use the property personally for extended periods, you value landscape and low-density development, your budget exceeds 300,000 EUR, you prioritize capital preservation over yield, or you are buying as a long-term retirement property.

**Buy in Costa Blanca South if:** Your primary goal is investment return, your budget is under 300,000 EUR, you want higher rental yields, or you are comfortable with higher-density development environments.

The scoring data on [avenaterminal.com](https://avenaterminal.com) reflects this reality — Costa Blanca North properties tend to score lower on the value and yield components but higher on location quality. Your personal weighting of these factors should drive your decision.

## Our Assessment

Costa Blanca North is worth buying in 2026 if your buying criteria align with what it offers: quality of life, constrained supply, and premium positioning. It is not the right choice if you are optimizing purely for financial returns. The data supports this clearly — and the best investment decisions start with understanding what the numbers actually say.
