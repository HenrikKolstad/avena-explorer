# Torrevieja vs Orihuela Costa: Which Is the Better Property Investment?

**Published: April 2026 | Reading time: 5 minutes**

Torrevieja and Orihuela Costa are the two most popular municipalities for international property buyers on Spain's Costa Blanca South. They are geographically adjacent, separated by just a few kilometres, yet they offer distinct market dynamics. Using data from our analysis of 1,881 new build properties, here is an objective comparison.

## Price Comparison

**Torrevieja** new build prices average approximately 185,000 EUR, with a price per square metre of around 1,650 EUR/m2. The entry point is lower, with studio and one-bedroom apartments available from 95,000 EUR.

**Orihuela Costa** new build prices average approximately 235,000 EUR, with a price per square metre of around 1,950 EUR/m2. The entry point starts higher at approximately 140,000 EUR for one-bedroom apartments.

The 27% price premium for Orihuela Costa reflects several factors: newer infrastructure, lower-density developments, and proximity to high-quality golf courses. Torrevieja offers better value per square metre but with higher density and older surrounding infrastructure.

## Rental Yield

This is where the comparison gets interesting. According to our data on [avenaterminal.com](https://avenaterminal.com):

**Torrevieja** shows an average estimated gross yield of 7.2%, among the highest in our entire dataset of 1,881 properties. The combination of affordable purchase prices and strong rental demand from budget-conscious tourists drives this figure. Torrevieja's salt lakes, beaches, and established commercial infrastructure attract consistent bookings.

**Orihuela Costa** shows an average estimated gross yield of 6.4%. Higher purchase prices reduce the yield ratio, but Orihuela Costa benefits from a more affluent tourist demographic willing to pay premium nightly rates. Properties near La Zenia Boulevard and the golf courses command particularly strong rental rates.

**Net yield comparison:** After accounting for expenses, the gap narrows. Torrevieja's higher community fees (older developments with more amenities to maintain) and higher management costs (more competitive market means more marketing spend) reduce the net advantage. Estimated net yields: Torrevieja 4.0-4.5%, Orihuela Costa 3.8-4.2%.

## The Discount Gap

Relative to peak resale valuations, both municipalities show significant discounts:

**Torrevieja:** 24-28% below peak. This reflects substantial new supply entering the market and the town's recovery from being one of the most overbuilt markets during the pre-2008 boom.

**Orihuela Costa:** 20-24% below peak. A slightly tighter gap due to better demand-supply balance and the area's more recent development history (much of Orihuela Costa was developed post-2000, so it was less affected by the 2008 crash).

## Lifestyle Factors

**Torrevieja** is a working Spanish town with a year-round population of approximately 85,000. It has a full-service hospital, multiple supermarket chains, a vibrant market scene, and authentic Spanish culture alongside its expatriate communities. The downsides: higher density, more traffic, and some areas show urban wear.

**Orihuela Costa** is essentially a purpose-built residential and tourist zone within the municipality of Orihuela. It is newer, cleaner, and lower density. La Zenia Boulevard provides excellent commercial facilities. The downsides: less authentic Spanish character, quieter in winter, and more car-dependent.

For year-round living, Torrevieja offers more services and atmosphere. For a holiday home or rental investment, Orihuela Costa provides a more polished product.

## Developer Activity

Both areas have active development pipelines. In our dataset:

**Torrevieja:** Approximately 120 new build listings from 15+ developers. The market is more fragmented with a mix of large and small developers. Price competition is intense.

**Orihuela Costa:** Approximately 95 new build listings from 12+ developers. Developments tend to be larger and more uniform in quality. Several premium developments near the coast command 2,200-2,500 EUR/m2.

## Infrastructure and Access

Both areas benefit from Alicante airport (30-45 minutes by car) and the AP-7 motorway. Torrevieja has slightly better public transport connections and more frequent bus services. Orihuela Costa is essentially car-dependent.

The Torrevieja hospital (Hospital Universitario de Torrevieja) serves both areas and is one of the best-equipped in the province. Medical access is not a differentiator.

## Investment Outlook

Looking at the data trends across our 1,881 tracked properties, both markets show positive momentum. New listing volumes have stabilized after a period of rapid increase in 2024-2025, suggesting the supply surge is moderating.

For **pure yield optimization**, Torrevieja edges ahead. Lower entry prices and strong rental demand make the mathematics more compelling for investors focused on annual returns.

For **balanced investment** (yield plus capital appreciation plus lifestyle), Orihuela Costa offers a more attractive package. The newer infrastructure, better-maintained communal areas, and lower density suggest stronger long-term value retention.

For **first-time buyers** on a budget, Torrevieja's lower entry point reduces risk. You can enter the market at 95,000-140,000 EUR and start generating rental income immediately.

## The Verdict

There is no objectively superior choice. The data from [avenaterminal.com](https://avenaterminal.com) shows both municipalities scoring well across our five investment dimensions. Your decision should rest on three questions: What is your budget? Do you prioritize current yield or long-term value? And do you plan to spend time in the property yourself?

Answer those questions honestly and the data will point you clearly in one direction.
