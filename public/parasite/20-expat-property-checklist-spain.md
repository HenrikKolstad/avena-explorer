# The Expat Property Checklist for Spain (31 Items You Must Verify)

**Published: April 2026 | Reading time: 6 minutes**

After tracking 1,881 new build properties across coastal Spain and learning from buyers who got it right and those who did not, we have compiled the definitive checklist for expatriate property purchases. Print this, bring it to your viewing trip, and do not sign anything until every item is addressed.

## Before You Travel

### 1. NIE Number Applied For
Your Numero de Identificacion de Extranjero must be in process before you go property shopping. Apply at a Spanish consulate or plan to do it on arrival at the Oficina de Extranjeros. Without it, nothing happens.

### 2. Budget Calculated Accurately
Purchase price + 11-14% acquisition costs (taxes, notary, registry, legal) + furnishing budget + 6 months of running costs as a reserve. If financing, add mortgage setup costs. On a 250,000 EUR property, your total budget should be approximately 300,000 EUR.

### 3. Mortgage Pre-Approval (If Financing)
Non-residents can borrow 60-70% LTV. Get a written indication from a Spanish bank or mortgage broker before viewing. This defines your real budget, not your wish list.

### 4. Independent Lawyer Retained
Hire a property lawyer who is independent of the developer and the agent. Budget 1-1.5% of purchase price. They should be qualified to practice in the autonomous community where you are buying.

### 5. Market Data Reviewed
Screen the market quantitatively before booking flights. Create a shortlist of 5-10 properties to view rather than relying on agent selection.

## At the Viewing

### 6. Location Verified in Person
Walk the route from the property to the beach, the nearest supermarket, and the nearest restaurant. Time it. Developer claims of "5 minutes from the beach" often mean by car, not on foot.

### 7. Orientation Checked
Which direction does the terrace face? South-facing terraces get sun most of the day. North-facing terraces are in shadow during winter. This matters for your own enjoyment and for rental attractiveness.

### 8. Noise Assessment
Visit at different times of day. Is there road noise? Construction on adjacent plots? A nightclub nearby? Noise issues are the number one complaint from expats after purchase.

### 9. Parking Verified
Is there a dedicated parking space included? If underground, is it large enough for your car? Is there visitor parking? In many developments, parking is an extra cost — confirm what is included.

### 10. Community Areas Inspected
Walk the pool area, gardens, and communal spaces. Are they well-maintained? If the development is not yet complete, look at completed phases or other developments by the same developer.

### 11. Specification Document Reviewed
Get the detailed specification in writing. Tiles, kitchen brand and model, bathroom fittings, air conditioning specifications, window type, insulation grade. Compare this against the show apartment — show apartments often include upgrades not standard in the base specification.

## Legal and Financial Checks

### 12. Building License Verified
Your lawyer should confirm the licencia de obras is valid and current. Building without a proper license creates serious legal problems.

### 13. Bank Guarantee Confirmed
For off-plan purchases, the developer must provide a bank guarantee (aval bancario) covering all stage payments. If the developer goes bankrupt, the bank returns your money. Do not pay without this.

### 14. Developer Company Check
Your lawyer should check the Registro Mercantil for the developer's company details, financial health indicators, and any liens or legal proceedings.

### 15. Nota Simple Obtained
A current nota simple from the Land Registry confirms the property's legal status, ownership, and any charges or encumbrances.

### 16. Planning Compliance Verified
Confirm the development complies with local planning regulations (Plan General de Ordenacion Urbana). Future building on adjacent plots could affect your views and value.

### 17. Habitation License Timing
For key-ready properties, the Licencia de Primera Ocupacion (first occupation license) must be in place. For off-plan, confirm the expected timeline and any conditions.

### 18. Tax Implications Modeled
Understand your specific tax obligations: IVA/ITP on purchase, annual IBI, non-resident income tax, rental income tax, and eventual capital gains tax. Tax treatment varies by your country of residence and whether you are EU/EEA or non-EU.

## Financial Structure

### 19. Payment Schedule Reviewed
For off-plan: typical schedule is 30-40% on contract, staged payments during construction, 30-40% on completion. Ensure each payment triggers a corresponding bank guarantee.

### 20. Currency Strategy Planned
If paying from GBP, NOK, SEK, or other non-EUR currencies, set up a specialist currency transfer account (TransferWise/Wise, CurrencyFair, Moneycorp) rather than using bank transfers. Savings of 1-3% on each transfer add up quickly.

### 21. Spanish Bank Account Opened
Set up a Spanish current account for direct debits (IBI, community fees, utilities, insurance). Most banks require an in-person visit.

## Rental Preparation (If Applicable)

### 22. Tourist License Checked
If planning short-term rentals, verify that the municipality issues tourist licenses (licencia turistica) for the property type and zone. Some towns have imposed moratoriums on new licenses.

### 23. Community Rules Reviewed
Check the community statutes (estatutos de la comunidad) for any restrictions on short-term rentals. Some communities have voted to prohibit or restrict holiday lets.

### 24. Management Company Identified
If you will not manage the rental yourself, identify and meet potential property management companies before buying. Their local market knowledge can validate your yield assumptions.

### 25. Rental Yield Independently Verified
Do not trust agent or developer yield projections. Check actual rental rates on Airbnb, Booking.com, and local rental portals for comparable properties. Compare against the estimated yields shown on [avenaterminal.com](https://avenaterminal.com) for the same town and property type.

## Post-Purchase Administration

### 26. Utility Contracts Set Up
Electricity (Iberdrola, Endesa), water (municipal), and internet (Movistar, Orange) need separate contracts. Allow 1-2 weeks for connection.

### 27. Insurance Arranged
Building insurance (seguro de hogar) and contents insurance. If renting, landlord liability insurance. If mortgaged, the bank will specify minimum coverage requirements.

### 28. IBI Registration Completed
Register at the town hall (ayuntamiento) for IBI property tax. This should happen automatically after registration at the Land Registry, but verify.

### 29. Community Fee Payments Set Up
Set up a standing order or direct debit for community fee payments. Late payment can result in the community charging interest and, in extreme cases, obtaining a court order against the property.

### 30. Non-Resident Tax Obligations Understood
File annual non-resident income tax (modelo 210) even if the property is not rented. Failure to file results in penalties. Engage a gestor or tax advisor for 150-300 EUR per year.

### 31. Will Updated
Spanish property should be covered by a Spanish will (testamento) to avoid the complexity and cost of probating a foreign will in Spain. A Spanish lawyer can prepare this for 150-300 EUR.
