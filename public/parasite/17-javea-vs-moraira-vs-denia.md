# Javea vs Moraira vs Denia: Which Costa Blanca North Town Is Right for You?

**Published: April 2026 | Reading time: 5 minutes**

These three towns along Costa Blanca North are frequently compared by international buyers. They are geographically close — Denia to Moraira is just 30 kilometres along the coast — but they offer distinct market dynamics. Using data from our tracking of 1,881 new build properties, here is an objective comparison.

## Price Comparison

Based on our current data at [avenaterminal.com](https://avenaterminal.com):

**Javea:** Average new build price approximately 345,000 EUR. Price per square metre: 2,900 EUR/m2. Entry point for a two-bedroom apartment: approximately 250,000 EUR. Villas start around 450,000 EUR.

**Moraira:** Average new build price approximately 420,000 EUR. Price per square metre: 3,100 EUR/m2. Entry point for a two-bedroom apartment: approximately 295,000 EUR. Villas start around 550,000 EUR.

**Denia:** Average new build price approximately 265,000 EUR. Price per square metre: 2,200 EUR/m2. Entry point for a two-bedroom apartment: approximately 185,000 EUR. Villas start around 350,000 EUR.

Denia is the clear value play, pricing 23% below Javea and 37% below Moraira on a per-square-metre basis. The question is whether the premium towns justify their higher costs.

## Rental Yield

**Denia: 5.5-6.0% gross.** The most affordable entry point delivers the strongest yield. Denia has a diverse rental market — Spanish domestic tourists, families, and long-stay visitors. The old town, castle, and port area attract cultural tourists beyond the standard beach holiday market.

**Javea: 4.8-5.2% gross.** Higher purchase prices suppress the yield ratio. Javea attracts premium renters but not at rates that proportionally offset the purchase premium. Peak season weeks command strong rates (900-1,200 EUR/week for a two-bedroom), but shoulder season demand is more modest.

**Moraira: 4.2-4.8% gross.** The lowest yield in this comparison. Moraira's exclusivity and tranquility — its key selling points — also mean a smaller rental market. The town is quieter, which appeals to lifestyle buyers but limits rental volumes.

For pure yield optimization, Denia wins convincingly.

## Character and Lifestyle

**Javea** is the largest of the three with a year-round population of approximately 27,000. It offers three distinct areas: the old town (historic, traditional), the port (restaurants, nightlife), and the Arenal beach zone (tourist-oriented). This diversity gives Javea the most balanced lifestyle proposition. The British and Scandinavian expat communities are well-established, with English-language services readily available.

**Moraira** is the smallest and most exclusive, with a year-round population of approximately 12,000. It is a fishing village that has evolved into a premium residential destination. The town centre is compact and walkable, with excellent restaurants and a small but attractive beach. Moraira appeals to buyers seeking privacy and tranquility rather than a bustling social scene.

**Denia** is the most authentically Spanish of the three, with a year-round population of approximately 42,000. It has a working fishing port, a Michelin-starred restaurant, a castle, and a vibrant local culture that does not revolve around expatriate communities. The ferry connection to Ibiza adds a unique dimension. Denia is where you go if you want to live in Spain, not in a Spanish version of Northern Europe.

## Market Dynamics

**New build supply:** Denia has the most active development pipeline, with approximately 15-20 new build projects currently tracked. Javea has 8-12 active projects, and Moraira has only 5-8 due to limited development land and restrictive planning policies.

**Resale liquidity:** Javea has the deepest resale market with the highest volume of transactions. Properties typically sell within 6-12 months at reasonable prices. Moraira's premium market is less liquid — selling can take 12-18 months, and the buyer pool for properties above 500,000 EUR is smaller. Denia sits in between.

**Price stability:** All three towns showed resilience during the 2020-2021 period. Moraira and Javea maintained prices best due to constrained supply, while Denia experienced a brief 3-5% softening before recovering quickly.

## Infrastructure

**Airport access:** All three are approximately 80-90 minutes from Alicante airport via the AP-7 motorway. Denia has the advantage of the Valencia airport connection (approximately 100 km), which provides access to additional flight routes.

**Healthcare:** The nearest major hospital (Hospital de Denia) serves all three towns. Private clinics with international staff operate in each town.

**Transport:** Denia has the best public transport connections, including regular bus services and the tram/train line to Alicante. Javea has reasonable bus connections. Moraira is essentially car-dependent.

**Commercial facilities:** Denia has the most comprehensive shopping and services. Javea's Arenal zone has good supermarkets and retail. Moraira has limited commercial facilities — larger shopping trips require driving to Javea or Denia.

## Investment Outlook

Looking at the fundamentals across our 1,881 tracked properties:

**Denia** offers the best risk-adjusted return for investment buyers. Lower entry prices, stronger yields, and genuine appreciation potential as the town's profile grows internationally. The ferry terminal expansion and ongoing old town renovation suggest improving infrastructure.

**Javea** offers the best balance of lifestyle and investment. You will enjoy using the property and it will hold its value. Yields are modest but the resale market is deep. It is the safest choice for buyers who are not sure whether they are buying for investment or lifestyle.

**Moraira** is a lifestyle-first purchase. If you have the budget and value exclusivity, privacy, and natural beauty, Moraira delivers. Do not buy Moraira expecting strong financial returns — buy it because you want to live there.
