# The Honest Truth About Rental Yields in Spain (With Real Numbers)

**Published: April 2026 | Reading time: 5 minutes**

Every property listing in Spain seems to promise rental yields of 8%, 10%, or even 12%. These numbers are fiction. After analyzing 1,881 new build properties across coastal Spain, we can tell you what yields actually look like — and why the gap between marketing claims and reality is so wide.

## Gross vs Net: The Number That Changes Everything

The average estimated gross rental yield across our dataset is 6.2%. That is the number before any expenses. Here is what happens when you account for the real costs of operating a rental property in Spain:

**Starting point:** 6.2% gross yield

**Subtract management fees:** Most owners use a property management company for short-term rentals. Fees range from 15-25% of rental income. At 20%, this reduces your effective income by 1.24 percentage points.

**Subtract community fees:** Average community fees for a new build apartment on the costa run 800-1,500 EUR per year. On a 250,000 EUR property, that is 0.3-0.6% of the purchase price.

**Subtract IBI tax:** The annual property tax (Impuesto sobre Bienes Inmuebles) typically runs 0.4-1.1% of the cadastral value, which is usually 30-50% of the market value. Effective rate on purchase price: approximately 0.2-0.4%.

**Subtract insurance:** Building and contents insurance for a rental property runs 200-400 EUR per year, or approximately 0.1-0.15%.

**Subtract maintenance reserve:** Budget 1% of property value annually for maintenance and repairs. New builds require less in the early years but this evens out over a 10-year holding period.

**Subtract vacancy:** Even well-located properties experience vacancy. A realistic occupancy rate for short-term rentals on the costa is 65-75% of available nights, but many agents calculate yield assuming 85-90% occupancy. The difference costs 0.5-1.0 percentage points.

**Net yield estimate:** 3.2-4.4%

That is the honest range for most coastal Spanish properties. The exact number depends on your specific location, management costs, and rental strategy.

## Where Yields Are Strongest

Not all locations yield equally. Using data from [avenaterminal.com](https://avenaterminal.com), here are the towns showing the strongest gross yields for new build properties:

**Top performers (7.0%+ gross):** Torrevieja, Los Alcazares, San Pedro del Pinatar, Pilar de la Horadada. These towns combine affordable purchase prices with established short-term rental markets. They attract budget-conscious tourists and long-stay winter visitors who provide more consistent occupancy.

**Strong performers (6.0-7.0% gross):** Orihuela Costa, Guardamar del Segura, Santa Pola, Gran Alacant. Slightly higher purchase prices but strong rental demand. Good airport access from Alicante drives consistent bookings.

**Moderate performers (5.0-6.0% gross):** Benidorm, Calpe, Altea, Fuengirola, Estepona. Higher purchase prices reduce the yield ratio, but these towns offer better capital appreciation prospects and longer rental seasons.

**Lower yielding (4.0-5.0% gross):** Javea, Moraira, Marbella, Nerja. Premium locations where purchase prices are high relative to rental rates. These properties are typically chosen for lifestyle rather than pure investment returns.

## Short-Term vs Long-Term Rental

The rental strategy dramatically affects your yield:

**Short-term (holiday) rental:** Higher gross yield potential (6-8%) but significantly higher costs and management complexity. Requires tourist license (licencia turistica), which is increasingly difficult to obtain in some municipalities. Cleaning, laundry, key handover, and guest management eat into margins.

**Long-term rental:** Lower gross yield (4-5%) but much simpler management. Tenants pay their own utilities, vacancy is minimal with a good tenant, and management fees are lower (8-10% of rental income). Spanish rental law strongly favors tenants, making eviction difficult and rent increases limited.

**Mid-term rental (1-6 months):** An emerging sweet spot. Winter lets to Northern European retirees (October through March) combined with summer holiday lets can yield 5.5-6.5% gross with moderate management complexity. This strategy works best in towns with established expatriate communities.

## The Variables That Actually Matter

Our analysis of 1,881 properties reveals that yield is driven more by location and property type than by any other factors:

**Property type:** Two-bedroom apartments consistently show the highest yields (6.5-7.2% gross on average). Three-bedroom villas with pools yield less (5.0-5.8%) because the higher purchase price is not proportionally offset by higher rental rates. Studios yield well on paper but vacancy rates are higher.

**Distance from beach:** Properties within 1 kilometre of the beach show roughly 0.5-0.8% higher gross yields than equivalent inland properties, driven by higher rental rates rather than lower purchase prices.

**Pool access:** Community pool access adds approximately 10-15% to rental rates but only 3-5% to purchase price, making it a genuine yield enhancer.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Do not trust agent yield projections.** They typically assume maximum occupancy, ignore expenses, and use the highest nightly rates achieved during peak weeks as the annual average.

**Do not assume your personal use weeks are cost-free.** Every week you use the property is a week of lost rental income. If you plan to use the property for 6-8 weeks per year, reduce your yield projection by 12-15%.

**Do not ignore regulatory risk.** Several Spanish municipalities are tightening short-term rental regulations. Check the licensing situation in your target town before buying. A property bought for rental yield that cannot legally be rented short-term is a very different investment.

## The Bottom Line

A realistic net rental yield for a well-chosen new build property in coastal Spain is 3.5-4.5%. That is competitive compared to most European property markets and significantly better than deposit rates. But it is not the 8-10% that agents promote. Buy with realistic expectations and you will not be disappointed.
